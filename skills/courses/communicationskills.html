Theoretical Foundations of Communication

Communication is a complex process that involves the transmission of information from one party to another. Theories such as the Shannon-Weaver Model highlight the importance of the sender, message, medium, receiver, and feedback in effective communication. Additionally, the Social Penetration Theory explains how relationships develop through communication, moving from superficial layers to deeper, more intimate levels.

Understanding these theories can enhance your ability to communicate effectively by recognizing the dynamics involved in interpersonal interactions.
